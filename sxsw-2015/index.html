SXSW 2015 in Review

I enjoyed myself a lot at SXSW 2015. I made it to a lot of sessions that were interesting and thought-provoking.

Friday

  • Welcome to SXSW Interactive by Hugh Forest
  • The Climate Crisis: Challenge and Opportunity by Al Gore
  • How to Rock SXSW in 4 Hours by Tim Ferriss
  • Fear, Shame, Empathy, & More Ways to Change Behavior by Dan Pink
  • Building Gender-Balanced Startups (Panel) by Sarah Buhr, Julie Ann Horvath, Stewart Alsop, Vanessa Camones

Saturday

  • OAuth2: The Swiss-Army Framework by Brent Shaffer
  • Patagonia: Why Business Is Good for the Planet by Rose Marcario
  • UFC: Women Breaking Ground Inside the Octagon (Panel) by Dana White, Ronda Rousey, Jessica Eye
  • Princess Reema’s Mission to Empower Saudi Women by Princess Reema
  • What it Really Takes to Win in Business by Jack Welch and Gary Vaynerchuk
  • Beyond Privacy: Surveillance’s Threat To Liberty by Schneier and Wizner

Sunday

  • Transgender in Tech: Past, Present, Future (Panel) by Allyson Dylan Robinson, Brielle Harrison, Kortney Ziegler, and Meghan Stabler
  • Be Epic: The Art of Bold Decision Making by Phil Libin
  • AI, Immortality and the Future of Selves by Martine Rothblatt
  • Data (in)Security: MIT Scientists Tackle Privacy (Panel) by Danny Weitzner, Ronald Rivest, Sam Madden
  • How to Fix American Politics by David Burstein

Monday

  • The Frontiers of Computational Thinking by Stephen Wolfram
  • Everything Eric Ries Has Learned Since 2011 by Eric Ries
  • Fixing Transportation with Humanity and Technology by Logan Green
  • Lessons from BuzzFeed by Jonah Peretti
  • Bitcoin: What It Needs to Succeed (Panel) by the Cameron and Tyler Winklevoss

Tuesday

  • Accelerators by the Numbers: 2014 Rankings by Susan Cohen and Yael Hochberg
  • Better and Faster: Predict Opportunity & Win in 2015 by Jeremy Grutsche
  • Holacracy: A Radical New Approach to Management by Brian Robertson
  • Moonshots and Reality by Astro Teller
  • How to Rob a Bank: Vulnerabilities of New Money by Josh Alexander, Bo Hartman, Bob Blakely
